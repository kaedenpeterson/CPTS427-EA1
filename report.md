For my activity, I wanted to gain experience with cybersecurity by participating in CTF challenges. The expectation was to complete an introductory learning course on TryHackMe and then attempt several CTF challenges with different levels of difficulty. Through this, the goal was to understand the thinking process that is needed to solve cybersecurity problems. I first completed the introductory learning course on TryHackMe to become familiar with the platform and to review basic cybersecurity topics. Then, I attempted an easy, medium, and hard CTF challenge.  

During the activity, I had to make technical decisions about how to approach each challenge. I started by reading the challenge description to try to figure out what type of problem it was, like cryptography or network analysis. Then, I chose tools and methods that seemed most useful. The challenges involved mostly things like Linux command-line tools, decoding encoded strings, or examining outputs. When I got stuck, I tried to break the problem up into smaller steps and tested different ideas until I found the solution.  

I completed the activity alone. I think my participation was strong. I spent time working through the learning material and the challenges instead of skipping difficult parts. Completing challenges at three different levels helped me see how the problems become more complex and require more critical thinking.  

If I were to do this activity again, I would spend more time documenting the steps I took while solving each challenge. This would help me review my process later and better understand which techniques worked best.
